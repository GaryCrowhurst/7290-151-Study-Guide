Maintenance Procedures

üõ¢Ô∏èEngine Oil Check & Change Procedure

  1. Park vehicle on level ground and apply handbrake
  2. Allow engine to cool (if recently run)
  3. Locate dipstick and check current oil level
  4. Assess oil condition (color, contamination)
  5. Identify correct oil grade from technical information
  6. Position drain pan under sump plug
  7. Remove sump plug and drain old oil completely
  8. Replace sump plug with new washer (torque to specification)
  9. Replace oil filter (prime new filter with clean oil)
  10. Refill with correct grade oil to specified level
  11. Run engine briefly and recheck level
  12. Dispose of old oil and filter according to regulations
  13. Record service in vehicle documentation
‚ö†Ô∏è Important Using incorrect oil grade can cause serious engine damage, increased wear, and voiding of warranties.

üíßCoolant System Check Procedure

  1. Ensure engine is completely cold before opening system
  2. Check coolant level in expansion/header tank
  3. Visually inspect for leaks at hoses, connections, radiator
  4. Check condition of coolant (color, contamination)
  5. Test antifreeze concentration with hydrometer/tester
  6. Inspect pressure cap condition and seal
  7. Check radiator fins for damage or blockage
  8. Test cooling fan operation (manual and automatic)
  9. Pressure test system if leaks suspected
  10. Top up with correct coolant mixture if required
  11. Test for combustion gases in coolant (head gasket check)
üö® SAFETY WARNING NEVER remove pressure cap from hot cooling system. Pressurized hot coolant can cause severe burns. Always wait until engine is cold.

üîãBattery & Electrical System Check

  1. Switch off all electrical consumers
  2. Inspect battery terminals for corrosion
  3. Check terminal connections are secure
  4. Test battery voltage with multimeter (12.6V+ when fully charged)
  5. Perform load test if battery age/condition uncertain
  6. Check alternator charging voltage (13.8-14.4V typically)
  7. Inspect drive belt condition and tension
  8. Test all lighting systems (headlights, indicators, brake lights)
  9. Check operation of wipers and washers
  10. Verify warning lights function correctly
  11. Test horn operation
  12. Check fuse box for blown fuses or damage

üõûTyre & Wheel Inspection Procedure

  1. Check tyre pressures (cold) against manufacturer specification
  2. Measure tread depth at multiple points (min 1.6mm legal)
  3. Inspect for cuts, bulges, cracks, or damage
  4. Check for uneven wear patterns (indicates alignment/suspension issues)
  5. Inspect sidewalls for damage or age deterioration
  6. Check valve condition and operation
  7. Inspect wheel rims for damage, cracks, or corrosion
  8. Check wheel nuts/bolts are secure (torque if necessary)
  9. Verify spare tyre condition and pressure
  10. Check tyre age from DOT code (replace if over 6 years old)
üí° Pro Tip Uneven tyre wear patterns tell a story: inside/outside edge wear suggests alignment issues, center wear indicates over-inflation, edge wear suggests under-inflation.

üîßBrake System Inspection

  1. Check brake fluid level in reservoir
  2. Assess fluid condition (should be clear, not dark/contaminated)
  3. Measure brake pad thickness (minimum 3mm typically)
  4. Inspect brake discs for scoring, cracks, excessive rust
  5. Measure disc thickness against specification
  6. Check for disc runout/warping
  7. Inspect brake hoses for cracks, bulges, or damage
  8. Check pipes for corrosion or leaks
  9. Test brake pedal feel and travel
  10. Check handbrake operation and adjustment
  11. Inspect ABS sensors and wiring
  12. Test brake warning lights function

‚ö°Hybrid/Electric Vehicle Maintenance Checks

  1. Verify vehicle is in "service mode" before working
  2. Check high voltage system is isolated if required
  3. Inspect high voltage cables and connectors (no damage/corrosion)
  4. Check orange safety warnings are visible and intact
  5. Test 12V auxiliary battery condition
  6. Verify cooling system for hybrid/battery pack operates correctly
  7. Check cabin air filters (critical for battery cooling in some vehicles)
  8. Test regenerative braking operation
  9. Verify charging system functions correctly
  10. Check AdBlue/DEF levels if diesel hybrid
  11. Scan for diagnostic trouble codes
  12. Ensure all safety labels are present and legible
üö® HIGH VOLTAGE SAFETY Only competent, trained personnel should work on hybrid/electric vehicles. High voltage systems (>60V DC) can be lethal. Always follow manufacturer isolation procedures and wear appropriate PPE including insulated gloves rated for high voltage work.

Safety & Legal Requirements

ü¶∫ Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

Essential PPE for routine maintenance:

Protection Type Equipment When Required
Eye Protection Safety glasses/goggles When working under vehicles, with fluids, or compressed air
Hand Protection Appropriate gloves When handling fluids, hot components, sharp edges
Foot Protection Steel toe cap boots Always in workshop environment
Respiratory Protection Dust mask/respirator When working with brake dust, solvents, or in dusty environments
Hearing Protection Ear plugs/defenders When using power tools or in noisy environments
Head Protection Bump cap When working under vehicles or in confined spaces
High Voltage Work Insulated gloves (rated) When working on hybrid/electric vehicle high voltage systems
üö® Critical Safety Rules
  • Never work under a vehicle supported only by a jack - always use axle stands
  • Always disconnect battery before electrical work (except testing)
  • Never open cooling system when hot - wait for engine to cool completely
  • Ensure adequate ventilation when running engines indoors (carbon monoxide hazard)
  • Keep fire extinguishers accessible and know how to use them
  • Never smoke or use naked flames near fuel systems or batteries
  • Report all accidents, near misses, and unsafe conditions immediately

üìã Legal Requirements

Key Legislation Affecting Vehicle Maintenance:

  • Road Traffic Act: Vehicles must be maintained in roadworthy condition
  • Construction & Use Regulations: Specify minimum standards for components
  • MOT Testing: Annual test for vehicles over 3 years old
  • Health & Safety at Work Act (HASAWA): Employer and employee duties
  • COSHH: Control of Substances Hazardous to Health
  • EPA: Environmental Protection Act - waste disposal requirements
  • Manual Handling Regulations: Safe lifting and carrying procedures
  • PPE Regulations: Provision and use of protective equipment
  • Electricity at Work Regulations: Safe electrical practices
  • WEEE Regulations: Waste Electrical and Electronic Equipment disposal

‚ö†Ô∏è Hazardous Substances - COSHH

Always read safety data sheets (SDS) for all chemicals used. Key hazards in routine maintenance:

  • Engine Oil: Skin contact can cause dermatitis - wear gloves
  • Brake Fluid: Corrosive, damages paintwork and eyes - handle carefully
  • Coolant: Toxic if swallowed - never siphon by mouth
  • Battery Acid: Corrosive - can cause severe burns
  • Brake Dust: May contain asbestos in older vehicles - avoid breathing
  • Degreasers/Solvents: Harmful vapors - ensure adequate ventilation
  • AdBlue/DEF: Irritant - avoid skin and eye contact

üîç Inspection Techniques

Technique Used For What To Look For
Visual Most components Damage, cracks, corrosion, leaks, wear, condition
Aural Running engines, bearings Unusual noises, knocking, grinding, squealing
Measurement Wear items, clearances Tread depth, disc thickness, pad thickness, belt tension
Functional Operating systems Lights work, brakes stop, wipers sweep, horn sounds
Pressure Testing Sealed systems Coolant system leaks, AC system integrity
Electronic Testing Electrical systems Voltage, current, resistance, fault codes

üìù Technical Information Sources

  • Manufacturer Workshop Manuals: Most authoritative, vehicle-specific procedures
  • Technical Service Bulletins (TSBs): Updates on known issues and solutions
  • Owners Manual: Basic maintenance schedules and specifications
  • Electronic Service Information: Autodata, Mitchell, Haynes Pro
  • Parts Catalogs: Correct part identification and specifications
  • Vehicle Identification Codes: VIN, paint codes, option codes, build dates
  • MOT Testing Manual: Legal inspection standards
  • Online Forums/Databases: Shared knowledge (verify from reliable sources)

‚è±Ô∏è Time & Cost Management

  • Work to agreed timescales to maintain customer satisfaction
  • Time directly affects labor costs and workshop profitability
  • Report anticipated delays to relevant person promptly
  • Keep customers informed of progress
  • Accurate time recording for job costing
  • Use estimated repair times from technical information
  • Balance speed with quality - rushing causes mistakes

‚úçÔ∏è Documentation & Record Keeping

  • Service Records: Date, mileage, work carried out, parts used
  • Job Cards: Customer details, vehicle details, work requested, findings
  • Inspection Checklists: Standardized checks, tick-off lists
  • Signature Required: Confirms work completed to standard, provides accountability
  • Warranty Records: Maintain for warranty claims, use approved parts/procedures
  • Additional Faults: Report any issues found outside requested work
  • Digital Records: Many systems now use electronic job cards
