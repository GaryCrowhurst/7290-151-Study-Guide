Vehicle Systems Explained

üîß Engine Systems

Four-Stroke Cycle

1. Intake: Piston moves down, intake valve opens, air/fuel mixture enters cylinder

2. Compression: Both valves close, piston moves up, compresses mixture

3. Power: Spark ignites mixture (petrol) or fuel injected into hot air (diesel), explosion pushes piston down

4. Exhaust: Exhaust valve opens, piston moves up, pushes burnt gases out

Engine Configurations

  • Inline: Cylinders in a straight line (e.g., 4-cylinder inline)
  • Vee: Two banks of cylinders at an angle (e.g., V6, V8)
  • Flat: Cylinders opposite each other, horizontally (e.g., Boxer engine)

üõ¢Ô∏è Lubrication System

Purpose: Reduce friction, cool components, clean internals, seal gaps

Components:

  • Sump (Oil Pan): Stores oil at bottom of engine
  • Oil Pump: Pumps oil around engine
  • Oil Filter: Removes dirt and metal particles
  • Oil Galleries: Passages that carry oil to bearings
  • Pressure Relief Valve: Prevents excessive pressure

Oil Types:

  • Mineral Oil: Refined from crude oil
  • Synthetic Oil: Chemically engineered, better performance
  • Semi-Synthetic: Blend of mineral and synthetic

üíß Cooling System

Purpose: Keep engine at optimal temperature (around 90¬∞C)

Main Components:

  • Radiator: Cools hot coolant using airflow
  • Water Pump: Circulates coolant through system
  • Thermostat: Controls coolant flow to maintain temperature
  • Cooling Fan: Pulls air through radiator
  • Expansion Tank: Allows for coolant expansion
  • Hoses: Connect components

Coolant (Antifreeze):

Mix of water and ethylene glycol. Prevents freezing (to -37¬∞C), prevents boiling (to 120¬∞C+), prevents corrosion, lubricates water pump.

‚ö° Ignition System (Petrol)

Components:

  • Battery: Provides electrical power
  • Ignition Switch: Controls system on/off
  • Ignition Coil: Steps up voltage to 20,000-40,000 volts
  • Spark Plugs: Create spark to ignite mixture
  • ECU: Controls ignition timing

Why Timing Matters: Spark must occur at exactly the right moment. Too early = pinging/knocking. Too late = power loss.

üöó Transmission System

Clutch (Manual Cars):

  • Flywheel: Bolted to engine crankshaft
  • Pressure Plate: Clamps clutch disc to flywheel
  • Clutch Disc: Friction material, connects to gearbox
  • Release Bearing: Pushes on pressure plate when pedal pressed

Gearbox Purpose:

Engines work best at certain RPM. Gearbox changes the ratio between engine speed and wheel speed:

  • Low Gears (1,2): High torque, low speed - for starting/hills
  • High Gears (4,5,6): Low torque, high speed - for cruising

üéØ Steering System

Types:

  • Rack and Pinion: Most common, direct feel
  • Recirculating Ball: Found in trucks, smoother but less direct

Power Steering:

  • Hydraulic: Uses fluid pump driven by engine
  • Electric (EPAS): Uses electric motor, more efficient

Steering Angles:

  • Camber: Tilt of wheel in/out at top
  • Caster: Tilt of steering axis forward/back
  • Toe: Whether wheels point in or out

üåÄ Suspension System

Purpose: Absorb bumps, keep tyres on road, provide comfortable ride, help handling

Types:

  • Independent: Each wheel moves separately (better ride, handling)
  • Non-Independent: Wheels connected by solid axle (simpler, stronger)

Components:

  • Springs: Support weight, absorb bumps (coil, leaf, torsion bar)
  • Shock Absorbers: Control spring oscillation, prevent bouncing
  • Struts: Combined spring and shock in one unit (MacPherson strut)
  • Control Arms: Connect wheels to chassis, allow movement
  • Anti-roll Bar: Reduces body roll in corners

üõû Tyres & Wheels

Tyre Construction:

  • Radial: Modern standard, plies run radially (90¬∞ to tread)
  • Bias/Cross-Ply: Old type, plies run diagonally
  • Tubeless: No inner tube, air sealed by bead on rim

Tyre Marking Example: 195/65 R15 91H

  • 195: Width in millimeters
  • 65: Aspect ratio (sidewall height as % of width)
  • R: Radial construction
  • 15: Rim diameter in inches
  • 91: Load index (max weight per tyre)
  • H: Speed rating (H = 130 mph max)

Legal Requirements (UK):

  • Minimum tread depth: 1.6mm across central 3/4 of tread
  • No cuts deeper than 25mm or 10% of section width
  • No exposed cords or ply
  • Correct inflation pressure

üõë Braking System

Disc Brakes:

  • Brake Disc: Rotates with wheel
  • Brake Caliper: Houses pistons and brake pads
  • Brake Pads: Friction material, squeeze disc to slow wheel

Drum Brakes:

  • Brake Drum: Rotates with wheel
  • Brake Shoes: Friction material, push outward against drum
  • Wheel Cylinder: Pushes shoes outward

Hydraulic System:

Pressing brake pedal pushes piston in master cylinder ‚Üí pressurizes brake fluid ‚Üí pushes pistons in calipers/wheel cylinders ‚Üí applies brakes

ABS (Anti-lock Braking System):

Sensors detect wheel lock-up. ECU rapidly releases and reapplies brakes (up to 15 times/second) to maintain steering control during emergency braking.

‚ö° Electrical System

Battery:

  • 12V lead-acid battery (6 cells √ó 2V each)
  • Stores energy chemically
  • Provides high current for starting (200-400 amps)
  • Powers accessories when engine off

Charging System:

  • Alternator: Generates AC electricity (converted to DC)
  • Drive Belt: Drives alternator from crankshaft
  • Voltage Regulator: Controls charging voltage (typically 13.8-14.4V)

Starting System:

  • Starter Motor: Powerful electric motor
  • Starter Solenoid: Engages starter to flywheel ring gear
  • Flywheel Ring Gear: Gear on flywheel that starter meshes with
